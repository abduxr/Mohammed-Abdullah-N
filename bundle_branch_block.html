Bundle Branch Block

User Details

Description

Bundle Branch Block (BBB) is a condition where there's a delay or obstruction in the electrical conduction within the heart's ventricles, specifically affecting the bundle branches, and is detected on an ECG by characteristic changes in the QRS complex. In Right Bundle Branch Block (RBBB), the right ventricle depolarizes late, leading to a widened QRS (>120 ms), an RSR "rabbit ears" pattern in leads V1 and V2, slurred S waves in leads I, V5, and V6, and sometimes T-wave inversions. In Left Bundle Branch Block (LBBB),the left ventricle depolarizes late, causing a broad or notched R wave in leads I, V5, and V6, a prolonged QRS (>120 ms), absent Q waves in certain leads, deep S waves in V1 and V2, and discordant T-wave inversions. RBBB can be benign but may also be linked to conditions like Pulmonory Embolism or Chronic lung disease, while LBBB is more concerning, often associated with serious heart conditions such as coronary artery disease, heart failure, or hypertension.Symptoms, if present, include dizziness, fainting, or palpitations, and treatment focuses on managing underlying conditions, with pacemaker implantation considered in severe cases.


Precautions

  • Regular Monitoring:
    • Schedule ECG check-ups to track heart function and detect any changes in the condition.

  • Manage Underlying Conditons:
    • Control heart-related issues like hypertension, coronary artery disease, and heart failure through medications and regular medical care.

  • Heart-healthy diet:
    • Follow a balanced diet rich in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean proteins to support cardiovascular health.

  • Exercise Regularly:
    • Engage in moderate physical activity, as recommended by your doctor, to improve heart health.


Effects

  1. Altered Heart Rhythm
    • Arrhythmias: Bundle branch block can lead to irregular heartbeats, increasing the risk of atrial fibrillation.

  2. Decreased cardiac output
    • Reduced Efficiency: The heart may not pump blood as effectively, potentially causing fatigue and weakness.

  3. Symptoms of heart failure

    • Shortness of Breath: Patients may experience difficulty breathing during physical activity due to impaired heart function.

  4. Increased risk of heart disease

    • Coronary Artery Disease: BBB may indicate underlying heart disease, elevating the risk for future cardiovascular events.
  5. Complications during surgery

    • Anesthesia Risks: Patients with BBB may have altered cardiovascular responses to anesthesia during surgical procedures.

  6. Potential progression to complete heart block
    • Complete Heart Block: BBB can progress to a complete block, leading to severe bradycardia and increased risk of cardiac arrest.

  7. Diagnostic challenges

    • ECG Interpretation: The presence of BBB complicates ECG readings, making it harder to identify other cardiac issues.

  8. Impact on physical activity

    • Exercise Intolerance: Patients may struggle with physical activity due to symptoms like dizziness or palpitations.
